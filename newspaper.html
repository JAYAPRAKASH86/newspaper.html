Space news

Human spaceflight:

SpaceX's Crew-10 returns to Earth: After nearly five months aboard the International Space Station (ISS), the Crew-10 astronauts are returning to Earth today, August 9. You can watch their splashdown live. Axiom-4 Mission returns: The Axiom-4 crew, including Indian astronaut Shubhanshu Shukla, returned to Earth today, marking the completion of the Ignis mission to the International Space Station. NDTV notes that this mission has opened up commercial horizons for India's space program. Jim Lovell passes away: NASA astronaut Jim Lovell, commander of the Apollo 13 mission and veteran of four spaceflights, died at the age of 97 on August 7, 2025. Blue Origin launches NS-34: Blue Origin successfully launched its NS-34 suborbital flight with six civilians on board, including Agra-born investor Arvinder Singh Bahal.

Lava planet:

Exotic 'lava worlds' are a hot new frontier in exoplanet science. Thanks to groundbreaking models and the James Webb Space Telescope, scientists are poised to explore their mysterious evolution and reveal clues about planetary formation across the galaxy.

NISAR Satellite Launch and Mission:

NISAR Satellite Launch and Mission: The NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar (NISAR) satellite was successfully launched on July 30, 2025. This collaboration between NASA and ISRO is designed to map Earth's land and ice surfaces, tracking changes to aid in disaster response and environmental monitoring. The satellite is currently in its commissioning phase. Satellite Images and Flood Relief Efforts: ISRO satellite images are being used to assess damage and assist relief operations following recent flash floods in Uttarakhand, India. TRACERS Mission: NASA's TRACERS satellites are studying solar wind interactions with Earth's magnetosphere.

Key launches

India's XPoSat: India's first dedicated polarimetry mission, launched on January 1, 2024, to study X-ray emissions from celestial objects. NASA's PACE: Launched in February 2024, the Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, Ocean Ecosystem (PACE) satellite studies ocean health, atmospheric particles, and climate interactions. China's Chang'e 6: Launched in May 2024, it aims to collect and return lunar samples from the far side of the Moon, offering insights into its geological history. It successfully landed on the Moon on June 1, 2024. Europe's Ariane 6: The maiden flight of this heavy-lift launcher took place in July 2024, aiming to boost Europe's capabilities for launching heavier payloads. However, the second stage failed to relight due to an auxiliary power unit anomaly. Japan's ALOS-4: Launched on July 1, 2024, it carries a L-band Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) sensor for observing disaster-hit areas, forests, sea-ice, and infrastructure displacement. SpaceX Starlink Launches: SpaceX continued its rapid deployment of Starlink satellites throughout 2024. However, a Falcon 9 suffered a rare engine failure during a Starlink mission on July 11, 2024, resulting in the loss of 20 satellites. Following the incident, the Falcon 9 was grounded by the FAA before resuming launches on July 27. NASA-ISRO NISAR Mission: A joint mission between India and NASA, NISAR was launched in early March 2024. It will create detailed 3D maps of the Earth's surface for monitoring natural disasters, managing resources, and studying climate change.
